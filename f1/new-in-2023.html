F1 SPRINT and Alternate Qualifying
This year, we'll have six sprint races in stead of three like last year. They'll be held in Azerbaijan, Austria, Belgium, Qatar, the United States (Austin) and Brasil. This means Qualifying will be held on Friday, setting the grid for the 100km sprint race on Saturday. The results of the sprint race will then be used to set the grid for the main race on Sunday.
During sprint races, DRS will also be activated after just 1 lap after the race start or restart, as a test to potentially implement this for all races in 2024.

Additionally, in two Grand Prix wekends yet to be announced, a diffrent Qualifying format will be used. All teams will be obliged to use hard tyres only for Q1, medium tyres only for Q2, and soft tyres only for Q3. This is meant to reduce to amount of tyre sets needed for a weekend, dropping the tyre allocation from 13 to 11 tyres per car.

New Tyre Compound Available
Pirelli has also introduced a new tyre set, making the total amount of available compounds now eight. Stating the jump between the C1 and C2 was too big, last year's C1 tyre is now known as the C0 and a new tyre has been introduced as the C1. Pirelli will now choose the soft, medium and hard compounds for each Grand Prix weekend from six diffrent compounds (C0 through 5), with the intermediate and wet compounds completing the line-up.

Additional Changes
That's everything that has changed for now, although an update release of the Sproting Regulations is still expected before the first Grand Prix in Bahrain. We might still see changes to the points system if the race is ended prematurally, as well as to the way grid-penalties for overuse of PU elements are applied and the way Parc Ferm√© works in Grand Prix weekends with sprint races.
